                                                               O R D E R

 

Justice R.S. Madan, President:

 

            The facts in brief giving rise to the present appeal are that the respondent-complainant had purchased a Maruti Car bearing registration No.HR-02H-3600 after obtaining loan from the appellants-opposite parties. In the registration certificate the Hire-Purchase entry was made in favour of the opposite parties.

            It is the case of the respondent-complainant that after payment of the entire loan amount to the appellants-opposite parties, he sought ‘No Objection Certificate’ from the opposite parties and also sought removal of the Hire-Purchase entry from the Registration Certificate. However, the opposite parties refused to issue N.O.C. for demand of Rs.21,600/- from the complainant. Thus, alleging it a case of deficiency of service and unfair trade practice, the complainant invoked the jurisdiction of the District Consumer Forum by filing complaint No.942/2005.

            Upon notice, the opposite parties appeared and resisted the claim of the complainant and while justifying the denial of issuing N.O.C. on account of non-payment of Rs.21,600/- by the complainant towards the loan amount, they prayed for dismissal of the complaint.

            On appraisal of the pleadings of the parties and evidence brought on record, the District Consumer Forum accepted the complaint by granting the following relief:-

“….we allow the complaint of complainant and directed the respondents to issue the NOC to the complainant without any charges and pay Rs.5500/- as litigation expenses. Order be complied within one month.”

            Hence this appeal.

            We have heard learned counsel for the appellants-opposite parties and perused the case file. None put in appearance on behalf of the respondent-complainant at the time of arguments despite repeated calls since morning. Keeping in view the heavy pendency of the cases in this Commission as well as the act and conduct of the respondent-complainant for non-appearance for the last three consecutive hearings, we do not think it appropriate to adjourn this appeal indefinitely.

            There is a delay of 54 days in filing of the present appeal the condonation of which has been sought by the appellants-opposite parties by moving an application which is supported with an affidavit.

            It is settled law that the delay cannot be condoned on the ground of equity and generosity but at the same time any illegality committed in the impugned order cannot be allowed to be continuing by ignoring the legal position on record. The present case being in similar situation, we condone the delay of 54 days in filing of the present appeal. 

            At the very outset the question for consideration before us is whether the controversy involved in this appeal is a “Consumer Disputes”.

            On behalf of the appellants-opposite parties it is contended by Shri D.K. Singal, Advocate that the respondent-complainant is holding the vehicle as a bailee under the hire purchase agreement and is not having any proprietary right or interest as a purchaser and since the respondent-complainant is not a purchaser of the vehicle, he cannot be termed as a “Consumer” as defined in the Consumer Protection Act, 1986 (as amended upto date).

            In support of his contention, learned counsel for the appellants-opposite parties referred to the decision of five bench judges of the Hon’ble National Commission in Revision Petition No.367 of 1998 “Tata Finance Limited versus Marjan Hossan and Ors., decided on 13.02.2003, wherein it has been held that:-

“The exercise of night by the (Appellant herein in accordance with the terms and conditions of the Hire Purchase Agreement cannot be considered as any  negligence on the part of the Opposite Party and the Appellant cannot also be branched as being guilty of any deficiency in service”. 

            In case law cited as RAM DESHLAHARA versus MAGMA LEASING LTD. III(2006) CPJ 247 (NC), has held that:_

”Moreover, under a hire-purchase transaction the financier does not render any service within the meaning of Consumer Protection Act, 1986 (for short the ‘Act’) and the petitioner is, thus, not a consumer. We do not find any illegality or jurisdiction error in the orders passed by Fora below warranting interference in revisional jurisdiction in Section 21(b) of the Act. Dismissed.”

            In case law cited as PADMA WATI versus TATA FINANCE LIMITED & ANR, II(2005) CPJ 31, Union Territory Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission, Chandigarh has relief upon the decision of the Hon’ble National Commission in Revision Petition No.367 of 1998, Tata Finance Limited v. Marjan Hossan and Others, decided on 12.02.2003 wherein it has been held:-

“On the point of the complainant being a consumer, we see that as per Clause 6 of the conditions attached to the agreement, the hirer (in this the complainant) held the vehicle as a bailee of the owners, and was not to have any proprietary right or interest as purchaser in view of this, he remained in the eyes of law what the agreement stated a hirer of the truck and not a purchaser of truck since he was not a purchaser of the truck as per law on the subject. It cannot be said that the complainant had hired the ‘services’ of the petitioner to fall within the purview of ‘consumer’ as defined in the Act.”

            The fact of the instant case are at par with the authoritative pronouncements relied upon by the appellants-opposite parties.

            Undisputedly, the complainant had got financed his vehicle with the appellants-opposite parties  as per Hire-Purchaser agreement and as such the dispute between the parties cannot be termed as a “Consumer Disputes”. The District Forum has not considered the factual position on record and as such the impugned order under challenge is not sustainable in the eyes of law.

            For the reasons recorded above, this appeal is accepted, the impugned order is set aside and the complaint is dismissed.

The statutory amount of Rs.2,750/- deposited at the time of filing the appeal be refunded to the appellants against proper receipt and identification in accordance with rules, after the expiry of period of appeal and revision, if any filed in this case.
