                                                               O R D E R

 

Justice R.S. Madan, President:

 

This appeal has been preferred against the order dated 11.07.2007 passed by District Consumer Forum, Sirsa in complaint No.463/2005 filed by complainant, whereby demand of Rs.40,000/- made by the respondents-opposite parties from the complainant on account of theft of electricity energy was held justified.

The brief facts of the present case as can be gathered from the record are that on 21.11.2005 the electricity connection bearing account No.TA-47/640 of the complainant was checked by Assistant Director Vigilance of the respondents (hereinafter referred to as the Nigam) in the company of other officials. During checking it was found that the welding set was being run by Prov L/C PVC Kundi LT line directly. At the time of checking one Balwinder Nath was present at the spot who signed the checking report alongwith the members of the checking party, in token of its correctness. As it was a clear case of theft of electricity energy, the opposite parties vide notice bearing Memo No.1767/CA dated 22.11.2005 demanded Rs.40,000/- as penalty from the complainant.

By filing the instant complaint, the complainant challenged the impugned demand by taking the plea that the checking was conducted in his absence; no notice was given to the complainant prior to the checking and also that the signature of his neighbourer were obtained by the checking party on blank printed performa and in fact there was no theft of electricity energy, as alleged by the opposite parties.

On appearance, the opposite parties justified the demand of impugned penalty in view of the checking report dated 21.11.2005 and prayed for dismissal of the complaint.

District Consumer Forum, on appraisal of the pleadings of the parties and evidence brought on record found no substance in complainant’s version and dismissed the complaint. Hence appeal.

 We have heard learned counsel for the parties and perused the case file.

Challenge to the impugned penalty as well as the impugned order passed by the District Forum while dismissing the complaint, is on the ground that no notice was issued to the complainant before conducting the checking and that the signature of a neighbourer of the complainant were obtained on blank papers and in fact there was no theft of electricity energy.

Undisputedly, it was a checking conducted by the Vigilance Staff of the Nigam the motive of which was to catch hold red handed the consumers who commit theft of electric energy causing loss to the Nigam. Therefore, in such a situation there is no need to issue notice to ‘consumer’ because by doing so, the officials of the Nigam cannot achieve the goal.  Though, the complainant has taken the plea in his complaint that his neighbourer Balvinder Nath, whose signatures were obtained on blank papers, had made representation to the authorities of the Nigam. But the complainant has failed to produce even an iota of evidence in this regard.  It is well settled law that where theft of electricity energy is apparent on record, the minor technicalities can hardly be of any significant. Reference in this regard is made to the observation made by the Hon’ble National Commission in Revision Petition No.3274 of 2010 Ashwani Kumar Versus Utter Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam Limited and others, decided on 03.12.2010  wherein it has been held that:-

“We have noted the submission only to be rejected because in our view, the factum of unauthorized extraction of energy was fully established from the material brought on record and the same cannot be discarded on certain technical assumptions.”

The facts of the instant case are fully attracted to Ashwani Kumar’s case (Supra).  It is not a case of dishonest abstraction of electricity energy but it is a case of taking direct supply from the LT line for running welding set. In a recent judgment the Hon’ble National Commission has observed that where the allegations against the consumer are for committing theft of electricity energy, the jurisdiction of the Consumer Fora is barred. Reference is made to case law cited as Ishwar Singh Versus Dakshin Haryana Vidyut Prasaran Nigam Ltd. 2011 CTJ 649 (CP) (NCDRC), wherein it has been observed that:-

“The Consumer Forums have no jurisdiction to go into the issues of theft of electricity by the electricity consumers.”

In para 3 of Ishwar Singh’s case (Supra), the Hon’ble National Commission has held as under:-

“No material has been produced before us by the complainant so as to come to the conclusion that the complainant is a consumer within the meaning of Section 2(1)(d)(ii) of the Consumer Protection Act, 1986, inasmuch as services for commercial purpose are excluded thereunder. Besides this in case of theft of electricity Consumer Fora have no jurisdiction to go into such issues. In view of this, we are of the opinion that the complainant, prima facie, is not a consumer within the meaning of Section 2(1)(d)(ii) of Consumer Protection Act, 1986 and the complaint before the Consumer Forum was not maintainable.”

The facts of the instant case fully attracted to Ishwar Singh’s case (Supra).

Having considered the facts and circumstances of the case and the legal position involved in this case, we do not find any illegality in the impugned order while dismissing the complaint. The opposite parties were justified in imposing penalty upon the complainant.  Hence, no case for interference in the impugned order is made out.

Finding no merit in this appeal, it is dismissed.
