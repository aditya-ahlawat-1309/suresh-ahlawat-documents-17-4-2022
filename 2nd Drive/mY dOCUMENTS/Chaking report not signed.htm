                                                               O R D E R

 

Justice R.S. Madan, President:

 

 This is an appeal against the order dated 10.03.2003 passed by District Consumer Forum, Rewari whereby complaint No.428/2000 challenging the penalty of Rs.78,115/- imposed upon the complainant vide notice bearing Memo No.6981 dated 15.11.2000, on account of dishonest abstraction of electricity energy, has been dismissed.

The brief facts of the present case as can be gathered from the record are that on 14.11.2000 the electricity meter bearing account No.USP-162 installed at the AARA MACHINE (Saw Mill) of the complainant was checked by the Vigilance Wing. During inspection it was noticed that the complainant was using un-authorized load of 11.190 KWs. The meter was tampered with and there were scratches on the meter body as well as on the glass of the meter. As per instructions issued by the Nigam-opposite parties, it was a case of dishonest abstraction of energy. Accordingly, the meter of the complainant was removed. Checking report was prepared at the spot which was signed by the members of the checking party, but the complainant refused to sign the checking report. In view of these facts, notice bearing memo No.6981 dated 15.11.2000 was served upon the complainant and letter No.6944 dated 17.11.2000 was sent to the S.H.O. concerned for registration of criminal case for abstraction of energy. F.I.R. was registered against the complainant.  Alleging it a case of deficiency of service against the opposite parties, the complainant filed the present complaint.

On appraisal of the pleadings of the parties and evidence brought on record, the District Consumer Forum dismissed the complaint.

Hence, this appeal.

We have heard learned counsel for the parties and perused the case file.

The impugned order of the District Consumer Forum has been assailed by the complainant with the averments that he had been paying the electricity consumption bills regularly and besides it he was paying illegal gratification to the Junior Engineer concerned every month and when he stopped the illegal payment to the said Junior Engineer, he was implicated in the present case.

In our view this contention of the complainant itself it sufficient evidence that the complainant was abstracting energy dishonestly for which he used to grease the palm of the Junior Engineer. If the complainant had been using the energy honestly, then the question of payment of any illegal gratification to the Junior Engineer does not arise. Thus, this admission on the part of the complainant in para No.3 of the complaint is sufficient to prove him dishonest intention with respect to abstracting the energy illegally in connivance with the officials of the Nigam.  

Another aspect in this case is that the checking was conducted by the Vigilance Wing of the Nigam and an affidavit of S.D.O. in this respect has been tendered in evidence as Ex.OP-1. Ex.OP-2 is the copy of re-connection order with respect to the electricity connection of the complainant.  It is also established on record that the complainant was present at the time of checking but he had refused to sign the checking report. This fact also goes against the complainant to show his dishonest intention.  Such a view has been taken by the Hon’ble National Commission in Revision Petition No.2047 of 2009, Uttri Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam Limited and another versus Karam Chand, Decided on 11-9-2009, wherein it has been held that:-

“In our view, in case the consumer refused to sign the inspection report and does not co-operate with the inspection team, no eye-brows can be raised against fairness of the inspection. That apart, if the respondent was aggrieved with the provisional assessment made by the Assessing Officer, it was open to him to file objection and eventually to move the appellate authority and Statutory Body, constituted under the Act notice, imposing penalty was also acknowledged by respondent. We do not feel impressed about any action resorted to by the respondent challenging either the alleged inspection or imposition of penalty, as required under the Act. The finding recorded by State Commission, as such, ignoring provisions of Electricity Act which the consumer was required to follow in case aggrieved, was not sustainable. The finding of the State Commission is, accordingly, set aside and revision petition succeeds. However, there will be no order as to cost.”

            The facts of the instant case are fully attracted to the case law cited above.

It is not the case of the complainant that the members of the checking party had any grievance against the complainant. The District Consumer Forum while deciding the complaint has considered all these aspects in its true perspective and as such no case for interference in the impugned order is made out.  The contention of the complainant that he has been acquitted in the criminal case, is of no help to him because in order to establish a criminal offence a higher degree evidence is required than that of the civil liability.  Reference may be made to case law cited as Punjab State Electricity Board vs. Surat Singh, IV (2007) CPJ 18 (NC), wherein it has been held that the theft of electricity is not to be proved like a criminal offence. It is a civil matter which is required to be decided on the basis of preponderance of probability, as the Consumer Fora exercises jurisdiction of the Civil Court.  The facts of the instant case are fully attracted to Surat Singh’s case (Supra).

As a sequel to our aforesaid discussions, it is well proved case of dishonest abstraction of electricity energy and as such no case for interference in the impugned order is made out.

Accordingly, this appeal is dismissed being devoid of any merit.
